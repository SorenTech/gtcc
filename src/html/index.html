GTCC Cancelling Spring Concert

Following the CDC and local Board of Health guidelines, GTCC has chosen to cancel its Spring Concert. We are eager to perform the Magnificat we have been rehearsing with an orchestra, and plan to do so at our next spring concert. This decision is out of an abundance of caution to protect our membership and our wonderful audience community.

Please stay in touch with GTCC through our Facebook page and our website. After this crisis passes, we look forward to creating ways to celelbrate our 40th season!

Beth Armstrong, Director

Beth Armstrong has taught at Roger Williams University, chaired the choral department at the Music School of the Rhode Island Philharmonic, and coordinated both the Junior Providence Singers and the JPS Treble Chorus.

    Beth is a graduate of Tufts University and pursued a master’s degree in Music Education from the University of New Hampshire. Prior to living in Rhode Island, Beth directed the Suncook Valley Chorale in Concord, New Hampshire and taught public school music for twelve years. In addition to directing our chorus, she also directs the Chorus of East Providence.

Heather Savage, Interim Accompanist

We welcome Heather Savage as interim accompanist. We bid a fond farewell to Melissa who’s life journey takes her out of state. Heather Niemi Savage grew up in Rhode Island, graduating from South Kingstown High School and the University of Rhode Island. She has extensive experience as a collaborative pianist which includes accompanying vocal and instrumental soloists and choral groups, performing in chamber ensembles, and playing in musical theater pits.

  • More...

    Her primary piano teachers were Dr. Donald Rankin and Dennis Hughes, from whom she learned classical and jazz piano, respectively.

    Heather studied composition under Dr. Larry Bell and Howard Frazien in Boston, MA.  In composition, as in life, she explores the interaction between the old and the new, tradition and innovation, examining every nuance and implication of her experiences and musical ideas. Composer Walter Saul has described her work as "stunningly resourceful with scant material."

    Her works have been performed in a variety of contexts including the national conference of the Christian Fellowship of Art Music Composers; the 2016 Rhode Island All-State Festival; the 2017 New Music Festival in Fitchburg, MA; and commemorative events at local churches. Additionally, her work has been featured on the radio program, "Music of Our Mothers." Her piece for string orchestra Daughter of the Stars, is a semi-finalist in the 2020 American Prize composition competition in the orchestral pops division and will also be released on Ablaze Records' Orchestral Masters Volume 7, due out in Spring 2020.

    In addition to her work as an accompanist and composer, Heather is the music director at Carolina Free Baptist Church in Charlestown, RI and maintains a private teaching studio.

We are a group of choral enthusiasts from Tiverton, Little Compton, Westport, Fall River, Aquidneck Island, and beyond. We gather to sing, promote choral music throughout our communities, and engage in community service. Since our first concert in December of 1980 we have been led by five wonderful directors, involved countless members, and performed almost 150 concerts. We hope you’ll consider joining us, whether to listen or to sing along!

“We all love to sing, but the chorus is about more than singing. It is a group of wonderful people, a way to give back to our community. It’s a family of like minded, kind hearted, talented people."

-Suzanne Rosevear, Chorus Member
